SVELTESITE BRAND BOOK
For flyers, Facebook posts, Instagram posts, and related design materials

Brand name
Svelte Design Studio

Brand spirit
Warm, calm, creative, human, and quietly memorable.
The visual language should feel artistic and professional at the same time.
Nothing should feel loud, aggressive, cold, or overly corporate.

Core brand mood
- Soft warmth
- Creative clarity
- Gentle confidence
- Handcrafted elegance
- Calm energy

COLOR USAGE RULES
- Lead with cream and other light backgrounds.
- Use sky, pink, and lavender as soft supporting accents.
- Use orange sparingly for attention points and calls to action.
- Use ink for most text and rust for stronger emphasis.
- Keep the palette airy and balanced; do not let one strong color dominate.
- Prefer soft blends and gentle contrast over harsh visual separation.

RECOMMENDED COLOR BALANCE
- 55% to 70% light neutrals and cream tones
- 15% to 25% sky or other cool-soft support color
- 10% to 15% pink or lavender accents
- 5% to 10% orange or rust for emphasis

TYPOGRAPHY

Current brand-safe font direction
Primary font:
Trebuchet MS

Fallback fonts:
Segoe UI, sans-serif

Typography feeling
- Clean
- Friendly
- Readable
- Lightly modern
- Not rigid

Typography rules
- Use bold or semibold for headings.
- Keep body text easy to scan and never cramped.
- Use sentence case or title case; avoid too much ALL CAPS.
- Use generous line spacing on flyers and social media graphics.
- Keep text blocks short and breathable.

Recommended hierarchy
- Main heading: bold, clear, warm, confident
- Subheading: medium weight, supportive, slightly smaller
- Body copy: regular weight, very readable
- Highlight words: use rust or orange, not too many in one layout

BRAND STYLE FOR LAYOUTS

General layout principles
- Use rounded shapes, soft containers, and gentle section breaks.
- Leave enough empty space around text and images.
- Favor layered softness over hard grids.
- When using backgrounds, soft gradients are preferred over flat harsh fills.
- Use subtle shadows only when they help separation.

Graphic style
- Soft organic shapes are welcome.
- Curves are better than sharp aggressive geometry.
- Use artwork, thoughtful photography, and elegant composition.
- If adding decorative elements, keep them light and airy.
- Visual rhythm should feel calm, not crowded.

Image direction
- Prefer authentic, warm, light-filled images.
- Use images that feel human, creative, and emotionally open.
- Keep image treatment natural; avoid over-editing.
- If text is placed over an image, add enough contrast for readability.

FLYERS

Flyer design rules
- Make the headline immediately visible.
- Keep the message focused on one main offer or event.
- Use one hero visual or one clean image group.
- Keep contact details and CTA easy to find.
- Use cream or very light backgrounds whenever possible.
- If the flyer is busy with content, reduce decorative accents.

Flyer do's
- Use clear hierarchy from title to details to CTA.
- Keep margins comfortable.
- Use rust or ink for the main text.
- Add orange only where you want the eye to stop.
- Keep important details grouped together.

Flyer don'ts
- Do not use too many font sizes.
- Do not place text too close to the edges.
- Do not use neon colors or black-heavy designs.
- Do not overload the page with multiple competing messages.
- Do not combine many unrelated image styles in one flyer.

FACEBOOK POSTS

Facebook post direction
- Design for clarity in-feed.
- The message should be understood quickly.
- Use layouts that work well in square or vertical crop.
- Headlines should be short and easy to read on mobile.

Facebook do's
- Use one main idea per post.
- Make typography larger than you think is needed.
- Use branded color framing around photos when useful.
- Keep the brand feeling warm and inviting.
- Include a clear CTA if the post is promotional.

Facebook don'ts
- Do not fill every area with text.
- Do not use tiny captions inside the visual.
- Do not rely on thin low-contrast fonts.
- Do not use overly salesy language visually.
- Do not create clutter with too many stickers, icons, or effects.

INSTAGRAM POSTS

Instagram post direction
- Prioritize visual harmony and a recognizable branded look.
- Posts should feel elegant and scroll-stopping without shouting.
- Use repeatable structure so the grid looks cohesive over time.

Instagram do's
- Keep compositions simple and polished.
- Use soft branded backgrounds or frames.
- Use consistent text placement from post to post.
- Let artwork, workshop images, or branded visuals breathe.
- Use accent color intentionally, not everywhere.

Instagram don'ts
- Do not overcrowd the square.
- Do not use too many decorative fonts.
- Do not overuse bright orange; keep it as an accent.
- Do not mix very cold colors with the warm brand palette unless there is a specific reason.
- Do not publish visuals that feel generic, harsh, or corporate.

DO'S
- Keep the tone warm, artistic, and trustworthy.
- Use the brand palette consistently.
- Maintain readable contrast.
- Choose calm compositions with clear focal points.
- Keep the brand feminine, creative, and professional.
- Use soft gradients, rounded edges, and thoughtful spacing.
- Design for mobile readability first on social posts.

DON'TS
- Do not use loud neon palettes.
- Do not use overly dark or heavy layouts as the default.
- Do not crowd the design with too much text.
- Do not mix many unrelated styles in one piece.
- Do not use sharp, aggressive visual language.
- Do not make the brand feel cold, trendy-for-a-minute, or mass-market generic.

VOICE AND FEELING
- Warm
- Personal
- Thoughtful
- Creative
- Calm
- Encouraging

The final feeling should always be:
Clear enough for business, warm enough for art, and soft enough to feel human.
